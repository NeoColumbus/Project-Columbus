# AI Disclosure

Full City Columbus is openly AI-collaborated.

AI is used for speed, scaffolding, provocation, drafting, rough design concepts, and meme or artifact generation.

AI is not a substitute for historians, planners, designers, lawyers, neighborhood residents, Indigenous voices, artists, transit riders, public servants, or anyone who actually lives the city.

## Ground Rules

- Everything AI-assisted is provisional.
- Humans should correct, localize, challenge, replace, or discard it.
- No fake grassroots.
- No fake people.
- No botnets pretending to be residents.
- No invented quotes from real people.
- No invented endorsements from neighborhoods, tribes, agencies, artists, or officials.
- No fake official affiliation.
- No historical claim should survive without human checking.

## Allowed Uses

- Drafting slogans.
- Sketching campaign copy.
- Testing flyer language.
- Exploring color, symbol, and layout directions.
- Summarizing public documents for later human review.
- Producing rough image concepts for real designers and residents to critique.

## Forbidden Uses

- Pretending AI output is local consensus.
- Replacing research with vibes.
- Speaking for Indigenous people.
- Inventing Columbus residents.
- Faking attendance, support, outrage, or neighborhood testimony.
- Hiding machine-made campaign artifacts behind fake humans.

## The Line

The machine can help us draft.

It cannot love Columbus for us.
