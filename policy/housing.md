# Housing

Build more homes.

Not as beige apology.
Not as displacement machine.
Not as dead frontage.
Not as luxury storage for absentee capital.

Build homes as city tissue.

## Position

Columbus needs more homes near transit, jobs, schools, groceries, parks, and ordinary daily life.

The city cannot become whole if growth becomes a private sorting machine.

Housing policy should make room for:

- apartments that meet the street
- missing-middle housing
- accessory dwelling units where they fit
- mixed-income buildings
- family-sized units
- preservation of existing affordable homes
- tenant stability
- public land used for public need
- small builders, not only giant repeat products

## Beauty Clause

More housing is necessary.
Dead housing is not enough.

Ground floors should have doors, windows, stoops, porches, trees, shade, and civic dignity.

Density deserves beauty.

## Source Hooks

The Columbus Housing Strategy says the region needs one home for every job to meet growth and repair the housing shortage.
