# Design Standards

<!-- HUMAN NOTE: Designers and architects should harden this into actual standards, examples, and anti-examples. Keep it usable. Keep it beautiful. -->

The capital should look like a capital.

That does not mean every building must be monumental.
It means ordinary life deserves care.

## Anti-Beige Minimums

- Real entrances on the street.
- Windows that make human sense.
- Ground floors with depth, not blankness.
- Materials that age with some dignity.
- Corners treated as public moments.
- Shade and weather protection.
- Service areas hidden without deadening the block.
- Signs that belong to the building.
- Parking that does not rule the site.
- Lighting that makes the sidewalk feel held.

## Materials

Brick.
Stone.
Wood where it can survive.
Metal with weight.
Glass used as an opening, not as corporate skin.
Color with discipline.

No beige surrender.

## Form Law

If a building takes from the street, it must give something back.
