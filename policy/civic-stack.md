# Civic Stack

<!-- HUMAN NOTE: This is the bridge from myth to politics. Keep it plain, local, and hard to dodge. Every claim that becomes public testimony needs source review. -->

Columbus is growing anyway.

The question is whether it becomes a pile of projects or a city.

The Full City answer is a stack:

Build the body.
Move the body.
Keep the body housed.
Make the body safe.
Power the body.
Give the body a center.
Open the body.

No single policy lane is the city.
The city is the stack working together.

## 1. Build The Body

Columbus needs homes at the scale of its growth.

That means:

- more homes near transit, jobs, schools, groceries, parks, and daily life
- Zone In beyond corridors where the city can actually handle more people
- public land used for public need
- faster approvals for projects that meet the city standard
- affordable housing bond money tied to units, stability, and repair
- family-sized units
- missing-middle housing
- small builders, not only repeat-product giants
- beauty standards that make density feel like city tissue, not storage

The line:

Build homes as city tissue.

## 2. Move The Body

COTA is not charity.

It is the city's nervous system.

LinkUS is the current spine: more service, BRT, better sidewalks, safer crossings, bikeways, trails, transit centers, shelters, COTA//Plus, and Mainstream improvements.

The standard:

- frequency is dignity
- the stop is a room
- the wait is a tax
- sidewalks are transit infrastructure
- night and weekend service count as civic structure
- bus priority is not optional where traffic chokes the body

The line:

Move the whole body.

## 3. Keep The Body Housed

No fake rent-freeze posture.

Ohio law limits local rent control. Columbus has to fight through the tools it can actually use:

- tenant counsel
- eviction prevention
- emergency rental aid
- habitability enforcement
- preservation of affordable units
- repair funds
- anti-retaliation enforcement
- community land trusts
- permanent supportive housing
- shelters connected to exits, not only beds

The line:

A city that grows while people fall out of it is not becoming whole.

## 4. Make The Body Safe

Safety is not a slogan and not a single department.

People should be able to feel safety in the street, on the bus, at the stop, outside the school, downtown at night, and inside their home.

The stack:

- visible public presence
- violence interruption
- domestic violence response
- youth rooms and after-school life
- lighting
- clean corridors
- safe crossings
- accountable policing
- fast repair of broken public space
- public data that tells the truth without flattening neighborhoods into crime maps

The line:

Safety is public form.

## 5. Power The Body

Growth runs on infrastructure.

Water, power, land, data centers, airport, logistics, hospitals, universities, and state capacity all have to answer the public body.

The city should welcome power that feeds the body.
It should reject extraction that drains the body.

The AI/data-center fight belongs here, and it gets its own file:

[ai-civic-dividend.md](ai-civic-dividend.md)

The line:

No silent extraction.

## 6. Give The Body A Center

Downtown cannot only be an office district, event zone, or skyline angle.

It has to become a civic living room.

That means:

- housing
- groceries
- transit
- late-night life
- riverfront energy
- public rituals
- street-level retail
- clean and lit corridors
- public restrooms
- benches and shade
- buildings that meet the sidewalk
- a capital city that looks like it believes in itself

The line:

The center should feel like the city has a heart.

## 7. Open The Body

The project itself should model the politics it wants.

Open source.
Novices welcome.
Public issue boards.
Templates.
Flyers.
Source notes.
Corrections.
Neighborhood modules.

Not everyone writes policy.
Some people make the flyer that gets the room full.

The line:

Open source civics is how the city learns to speak back.

## Public Message

Do not say:

- comprehensive stakeholder framework
- strategic alignment
- inclusive growth ecosystem
- urban innovation agenda

Say:

Columbus is growing anyway.

The question is whether it becomes a pile of projects or a city.

Build the body.
Move the body.
Keep the body housed.
Make the body safe.
Power the body.
Give the body a center.
Open the body.
