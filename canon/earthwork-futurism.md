# Earthwork Futurism

<!-- HUMAN NOTE: This is the design soul file. Use the references as energy, not imitation. Do not copy protected logos, mascots, or poster systems. Build an original civic language from Columbus, Ohio, ancient geometry, transit, water, power, and public myth. -->

Earthwork Futurism is the house style.

Old ground.
New machine.
Public myth you can wear.

It is not retro for retro's sake.
It is not sterile futurism.
It is not museum earth tones.
It is not tech-bro chrome.

It is old ground pressure translated into modern civic signal.

## The Formula

Earthwork form.
Transit line.
Public manual.
Street poster.
Flag logic.
Fan object.

If it cannot live on a flag, a sticker, a bus shelter, a sweatshirt, a phone avatar, a field guide, and a public hearing slide, it is not strong enough yet.

## Reference Energy

Use the references as design physics, not as things to copy.

Chicago flag:

- simple enough to draw from memory
- civic pride without explanation
- symbol as public property

1970s national parks and NASA:

- public-system seriousness
- disciplined grids
- thick color fields
- charts, badges, manuals, mission patches
- optimism without softness

Street propaganda:

- repetition
- high contrast
- one symbol until it becomes unavoidable
- poster as public pressure

Mascot-level affection:

- a form people want to wear
- a mark people can love
- a souvenir that feels like belonging
- no borrowed mascot, no fake character unless the project earns one

## Visual Laws

1. Make the symbol do work.
2. Use fewer shapes, harder.
3. Prefer circle, line, axis, ring, field, block.
4. Make every composition printable in two or three colors.
5. Make every line readable at street distance.
6. Use public-manual discipline, then break it with heat.
7. Let the flag lead.
8. Keep the palette civic, not lifestyle.
9. Make the artifact feel found, official, and illegal at the same time.
10. Never make it beige.
11. Put a real place into the composition.
12. Let the object feel handled, posted, carried, or reported.

## Inhabited Feel

Earthwork Futurism is not clean-room branding.

It should feel like:

- a sticker on a laptop
- a flyer taped to a window
- a bus stop field card
- a handbill folded in a pocket
- a public hearing slide with teeth
- a wall report from a named block
- a flag seen from a bridge

Use place names as voltage:

- Morse Road
- Parsons Avenue
- Broad and High
- Franklinton
- Linden
- The Hilltop
- Northland
- South Side
- Scioto
- Olentangy

Do not make the city into scenery.
Make the place the proof.

## Typography

Use heavy condensed type for public commands.

Use small technical sans text for labels, coordinates, file names, issue numbers, and source notes.

The rhythm should feel like:

poster headline,
manual caption,
field note,
chant.

## Marketing Law

Do not explain first.

Place first.
Signal second.
Then line.
Then demand.
Then proof.
Then source.

Order:

1. the named place
2. the mark
3. the impossible-to-ignore line
4. the civic demand
5. the proof path
6. the source trail

If a line could belong to any city, it does not belong here yet.

## Irresistible Test

Ask:

- Would someone put this on a laptop?
- Would someone wear it without needing a speech?
- Would a teenager steal the sticker?
- Would a planner recognize the demand?
- Would a bus rider understand the insult being named?
- Would a city official feel pressure?
- Would it still work in black and white?
- Would it still work huge?
- Would it still work tiny?

If yes, it has soul.
