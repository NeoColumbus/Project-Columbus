## Primary Flag: Second Geometry

The primary Full City Columbus flag is the Second Geometry flag.

River navy field.
Civic axes.
Limestone ring.
Transit amber core.

No skyline.
No mascot.
No explorer.
No seal clutter.
No fake ancient costume.

The flag should be simple enough to recognize from a porch, a jacket patch, a sticker, a bus shelter, a phone screen, or a cheap flyer.

## Meaning

The navy field is civic night, river depth, and the old place beneath the modern city.

The crossed axes are street, river, transit, and decision:

- Broad and High
- Scioto and Olentangy
- line and crossing
- movement and center

The limestone ring is gathering.

The amber core is the signal:

transit,
fire,
arrival,
public energy,
the city waking up.

## Construction

Use a 3:2 flag ratio.

The field is River Navy.
The main axes are Limestone.
The center rule is Bronze.
The core is Transit Amber.

## Usage

Use the flag when the project needs to feel civic, public, and durable:

- site branding
- print posters
- stickers
- banners
- social headers
- neighborhood modules
- public comment kits
- open-source repo identity

Do not over-explain it on the wall.

Let the shape carry.
