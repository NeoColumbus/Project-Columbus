# Governance

<!-- HUMAN NOTE: This project is young. Keep governance light, clear, and strong enough to protect the work without turning it into bureaucracy. -->

Full City Columbus is open source civic work.

The project is founder-led for now and contributor-shaped as real people start carrying parts of it.

## Decision Standard

A change is stronger when it makes the project:

- more Columbus
- more accurate
- more usable
- more beautiful
- more printable
- more buildable
- less fake
- less beige

Taste matters.
Sources matter.
Street usefulness matters.

## Maintainer Roles

Maintainer roles can be split as the project grows:

- Myth and language
- Research and sources
- Visual system
- Street kit and print
- Site and code
- Policy stack
- Community and issues

One person can hold more than one role early.
No role is permanent by default.

## How Decisions Happen

Small fixes:
maintainers can merge when the change is clean and improves the work.

Factual corrections:
prefer the better source. Mark uncertainty when needed.

Visual changes:
ship only when the asset survives scale, mobile, cheap print, and the no-collision test.

Big direction changes:
open an issue first, show the artifact or argument, and let maintainers decide after review.

## What Gets Rejected

- fake official affiliation
- unsourced historical claims
- stolen images or assets
- bot movement
- generic civic-brand filler
- policy with no material demand
- design that cannot survive street distance
- attacks on contributors instead of the work

## AI Use

AI can draft, scaffold, remix, and accelerate.

AI does not get final authority.

If AI helped create a contribution, say so when it matters. Check facts, image rights, local claims, and tone before asking the public to carry it.

## Becoming A Maintainer

A maintainer should have a trail:

- useful contributions
- good judgment in issues or reviews
- respect for sources
- respect for the city
- ability to improve the work without draining its force

Open an issue or ask an existing maintainer when the project is ready for a new hand on the wheel.
