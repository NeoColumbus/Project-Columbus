00 / why this exists

A city this old should not feel like spare parts.

Columbus has rivers, earthwork memory, state power, immigrant streets, suburbs, hospitals, colleges, warehouses, towers, buses, empty walls, and fragments of greatness everywhere.

What it lacks is the shared signal. Too often Columbus feels like annexed suburbs orbiting a couple blocks of downtown. That is not enough. The whole body has to start moving.

Policy matters: homes, transit, water, power, data, public beauty. But first the city needs an ethos people can feel, repeat, and improve.

No money. No committee. Open source. Go get the city.

This started as a passion project from someone who grew up in the shadow of Columbus and got tired of watching the city undersell itself.

The first move is not a perfect plan. It is a repo, a mark, a stack of posters, and an invitation: take the signal, make it sharper, and send it back.

08 / civic stack

Growing anyway.
Choose city.

Columbus is growing anyway. The question is whether it becomes a pile of projects or a city.

Read the stack
  1. 01 Build the body. Homes, density, approvals, public land, no small-town zoning costume.
  2. 02 Move the body. LinkUS, COTA, bus shelters, sidewalks, frequency, night service.
  3. 03 Keep the body housed. Tenant counsel, preservation, repair, habitability, eviction defense.
  4. 04 Make the body safe. Visible safety, violence interruption, light, youth rooms, clean corridors.
  5. 05 Power the body. Water, grid, data centers, public benefit, no silent extraction.
  6. 06 Give the body a center. Downtown as civic living room, not an office district in recovery.
  7. 07 Open the body. Open source civics, public issue boards, flyers, novices welcome.

09 / AI civic dividend

If the machine lives here, it pays tribute to the city.

AI is not magic. It is water, land, power, labor, and data.

Read the dividend
  • Water share Public reporting, recycled cooling, drought plans, no hidden draw.
  • Power share Data centers pay for the grid they force into existence.
  • Land share No dead boxes on civic ground without stormwater, trees, design, and repair.
  • Tax share If public revenue is waived, housing, transit, and water get paid back.
  • Compute share Cloud credits for schools, libraries, artists, nonprofits, and local builders.
  • Data share Public data belongs in a privacy-first commons, not a black-box empire.
