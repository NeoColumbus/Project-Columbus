# COTA Is The Nervous System

<!-- HUMAN NOTE: Riders should rewrite this. Add lived route notes, stop conditions, missed transfer pain, and actual places where better service would change daily life. -->

COTA is not charity.

It is not a pity machine.
It is not backup transportation for people with fewer choices.

COTA is the city's nervous system.

Transit is how Columbus recognizes itself across distance.
Transit is how a capital admits that its people move in more than one way.
Transit is how a city becomes available to itself.

## Key Line

COTA is not charity. It is the city's nervous system.

## The Claim

A city that treats transit as optional is a city that does not believe in its own body.

Bus stops are civic organs.
Frequency is dignity.
Shelter is dignity.
Night service is dignity.
Straight routes are dignity.
Reliable transfers are dignity.

## Demand

Treat transit as public structure, not private misfortune.

## What This Builds

This campaign makes transit emotionally obvious.

Do not argue for buses like a spreadsheet begging for sympathy.
Argue for transit like a city admitting it has limbs.

COTA is how a worker reaches a shift.
COTA is how a student reaches a library.
COTA is how a senior reaches an appointment.
COTA is how a downtown becomes available without a parking ritual.
COTA is how neighborhoods stop living as separate islands.

## Public Demands

- More frequency on major routes.
- Shelters that act like rooms, not poles in weather.
- Safe crossings to every serious stop.
- Night and weekend service that matches real work.
- Bus priority where traffic strangles service.
- Better transfer points.
- Clear signage and real-time information.
- LinkUS built as civic spine, not just project delivery.

## Route Notes To Add

Every route page or flyer should answer:

- What shifts does this route serve?
- Where does it fail at night?
- Which stops need shelter?
- Which crossings are hostile?
- Which transfer hurts the most?
- What neighborhood becomes more whole if this route improves?

## First Builds

- Make a flyer for one route.
- Make a bus-stop scorecard.
- Photograph three stops that insult riders.
- Photograph three stops that almost work.
- Write a public comment template for shelter, frequency, and crossing upgrades.
