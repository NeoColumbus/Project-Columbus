# Print Notes

Flyers should work before they look precious.

## Rules

- Must survive black-and-white printing.
- Must read from six feet away.
- Must not depend on a phone screen.
- Must include no fake official seal.
- Must leave room for local notes, route numbers, meeting dates, or hand-scrawled corrections.
- Must avoid tiny light text on dark backgrounds unless the contrast is brutal.
- Must be printable on letter paper first.

## Recommended Formats

- Letter portrait for wall posting.
- Letter landscape for bus-stop handouts.
- Half-letter for stacks on counters.
- One-color stencil versions for cheap reproduction.

## Materials

Use bone paper if possible.
Use black, river navy, brick, or bronze ink.
Use transit amber only when it can reproduce clearly.

No glossy fake luxury.
No beige retreat.
