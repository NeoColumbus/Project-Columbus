## Public Action

Post the signal.

## What This Drop Is Doing

Drop 001 introduces the signal mark and turns the project from a manifesto into a public artifact system.

The goal is not to explain Full City Columbus in one paragraph.

The goal is to make the symbol show up, make people ask what it means, and give them a clean way to add their own neighborhood pressure.

## Evidence Posters

The posters should feel less like slogans and more like field evidence:

- Signal seen: confluence.
- The earthworks were first.
- The center was here before the seal.
- A pile of projects is not a city.
- The machine pays tribute.
- Report a dead wall.

## Civic Demands

Second Geometry means the city has to take form:

- housing that lets the body grow
- transit that lets the body move
- public beauty that lets the body feel itself
- water, power, land, and data handled as public matters
- open-source civic work people can actually join

## Participation Loop

1. Post or print the signal.
2. Attach it to one local truth.
3. Name what is broken or unfinished.
4. Point to one buildable demand.
5. Send it back as a pull request, issue, flyer, photo, caption, or field note.

## First Missions

- Put the signal on a social avatar for one week.
- Print one poster and photograph it in context.
- Make one neighborhood version: `PLACE is not a fragment.`
- Score one bus stop.
- Report one dead wall.
- Write one data center public test.
- Add one better Columbus source.

## The Rule

Do not make people read the whole theory before they can feel the city.

Signal first.
Action second.
Source trail third.
