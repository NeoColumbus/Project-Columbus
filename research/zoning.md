## Working Frame

Zoning is hidden city theology.

It says where people may live.
It says how many doors can exist.
It says whether a street can hold a corner store.
It says whether the future arrives as a neighborhood or a spreadsheet.

## Current Research Hook

Columbus has been updating its zoning code through Zone In. The official Zone In site says the first 2024 zoning code iteration, Title 34, created new districts and rules for those districts.

## Questions

- Where does the new code allow more homes?
- Where does it still preserve scarcity?
- Which corridors are treated as future city?
- Which neighborhoods remain frozen?
- What design rules prevent dead frontage?
- Where are transit and housing finally speaking to each other?
