# Comparative Cities

<!-- HUMAN NOTE: Keep comparisons honest. Do not pretend Columbus is New York, Chicago, or Philadelphia. Study what whole-city feeling means, then translate without cosplay. -->

## Working Frame

Some cities can make people feel the whole body waking up.

New York does it through density, myth, money, transit, noise, media, and brutal public presence.

Chicago does it through lake, grid, towers, neighborhoods, trains, weather, labor memory, and civic self-belief.

Philadelphia does it through age, street life, rowhouses, murals, sports pain, history, transit, and refusal to apologize.

Columbus should not copy them.

Columbus should learn from the fact that they are legible.

## Questions For Columbus

- What is our equivalent of whole-city presence?
- Where does Columbus already feel like itself?
- Where does it become generic?
- Which rituals are city-scale and which are only district-scale?
- What would make the capital visible as a capital?
- What would make the bus feel like part of the myth?
- What would make new housing feel like city tissue, not product?
