# Credibility Queue

<!-- HUMAN NOTE: This is where myth gets made harder. Public copy can stay sharp. Claims that become policy, testimony, or high-visibility history need proof. -->

Use this queue when a claim moves from campaign language into public argument.

## P0: Must Source Before Public Testimony

### Columbus Growth And Annexation

- Claim: Columbus behaves like a major city stitched from annexed fragments.
- Needs: annexation timeline, population geography, municipal boundary maps, neighborhood examples.
- Owner needed: local historian, planner, GIS person, resident reviewers.

### Earthworks And Civic Geometry

- Claim: central Ohio already held monumental public form before the modern city.
- Needs: Hopewell / Newark / central Ohio earthwork citations, archaeological review, language review.
- Owner needed: historian or archaeologist, and living Native review before high-visibility use.

### Transit As Civic Nervous System

- Claim: COTA service gaps keep Columbus from moving like one body.
- Needs: route maps, headways, service hours, LinkUS status, rider accounts, stop-condition photos.
- Owner needed: riders first, transit advocate second.

### Housing And Fragmentation

- Claim: Columbus needs housing that lets the whole body grow without dead walls and beige churn.
- Needs: zoning citations, permit data, rent burden, infill examples, anti-displacement examples.
- Owner needed: housing researcher, renter, builder, neighborhood reviewer.

### AI Civic Dividend

- Claim: data centers use public land, water, power, tax structure, and local data, so the public deserves a share.
- Needs: Ohio data center incentives, utility load, water use, tax abatements, procurement models, legal review.
- Owner needed: energy lawyer, utility watcher, public finance person, data governance person.

## P1: Needs Local Eyes

- Dead wall examples by corridor.
- Bus stop scorecards by route.
- Blank public realm photos with exact location.
- Neighborhood fragment lines written by people who live there.
- Slogan tests from actual posters, stickers, and social posts.

## P2: Kill Or Harden

Cut any claim that cannot survive correction.

Harden any line people keep repeating.

Never let a weak source carry a strong poster.
