## Working Frame

The legal city is not the whole city.

The City of Columbus traces its founding to 1812 near the confluence of the Scioto and Olentangy rivers. City materials note that Columbus was created specifically to be Ohio's capital and became the permanent capital in 1816.

That is the legal and institutional story.

The deeper story includes the rivers, routes, Indigenous histories, Franklinton, state power, Black neighborhoods, immigrant life, annexation, universities, hospitals, highways, warehouses, suburbs, arts scenes, churches, bars, union halls, schools, and bus stops.
