## Working Notes

The Hopewell Ceremonial Earthworks are ancient Indigenous earthwork sites in Ohio. UNESCO describes them as monumental earthen enclosure complexes built between about 2,000 and 1,600 years ago along central tributaries of the Ohio River.

The National Park Service describes the earthworks as ceremonial landscapes used for feasts, funerals, rituals, and rites of passage.

NPS notes that Ohio's Hopewell Ceremonial Earthworks became the United States' 25th UNESCO World Heritage listing on September 19, 2023.
